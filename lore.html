The Greater Will & The Cosmic Order

The Greater Will is a mysterious cosmic entity that serves as the dominant divine force in the Lands Between. It hails from a "lightless void" and operates through emissaries rather than direct intervention. According to the Three Fingers, the Greater Will fractured the primordial "One Great"—a unified existence from which "all that there is came"—creating individuality, souls, and the world itself.

The Greater Will sent multiple emissaries to the Lands Between. Metyr, Mother of Fingers was described as a "magnificently gleaming daughter" and the first shooting star to fall upon the world. She spawned the Two Fingers—physical entities that communicate through a "language of light" requiring special interpreters to understand. The Elden Beast arrived later, carried by a golden star—it would later become the Elden Ring itself.

The Elden Ring is not merely a physical object but a living incarnation of Order that defines the fundamental laws of reality. Its interlocking runes represent concepts like life, death, causality, and regression. The Elden Beast serves as both the Ring and its guardian—the living incarnation of the concept of Order.

The Goddess-Queen

Queen Marika was a Numen woman from Shaman Village in what would become the Land of Shadow. The Numen were an extremely long-lived race from outside the Lands Between.

The Shadow of the Erdtree DLC reveals her traumatic origins: the Hornsent civilization that ruled her homeland systematically tortured her people, discovering that Shaman flesh "mended harmoniously with others" and using them to create Living Jars through horrific rituals.

Chosen as an Empyrean by the Two Fingers, Marika traveled to the Gate of Divinity atop Enir-Ilim where she became the vessel of the Elden Ring. Her first major act as goddess was removing the Rune of Death, eliminating natural mortality for herself and her bloodline.

"O Radagon, leal hound of the Golden Order. Thou'rt yet to become me. Thou'rt yet to become a god. Let us be shattered, both. Mine other self."
— Queen Marika's Spoken Echo

The game's central mystery—"Radagon is Marika"—reveals they are the same being with two distinct personalities and often opposing wills. This duality mirrors the alchemical Rebis, a being possessing both male and female qualities. When Marika shattered the Elden Ring, Radagon attempted to repair it.

The Demigod Shardbearers

Marika's first consort was Godfrey (originally Hoarah Loux), a savage warrior chieftain. Together they produced Godwyn the Golden, and the cursed Omen twins Morgott and Mohg. Her second consort was Radagon—her own other half. Before returning to Marika, Radagon had married Rennala, Queen of the Full Moon, producing Ranni, Radahn, and Rykard. Marika and Radagon's union produced Malenia and Miquella—both born as Empyreans.

Ranni the Witch - Mastermind

On a cold, wintry night during the Golden Age, Black Knife Assassins infiltrated Leyndell and murdered Godwyn the Golden—the first recorded death of a demigod.

The mastermind was Ranni the Witch, who resented being chosen by the Two Fingers as Marika's potential successor. She stole a fragment of the Rune of Death from Maliketh and performed a fearsome rite to imbue it into the Black Knives.

At the exact moment Godwyn was killed, Ranni killed her own physical body. This split death created the Cursemark: Godwyn's soul died while his body lived; Ranni's body died while her soul survived.

Godwyn's soulless body, buried at the Erdtree's roots, transformed into the grotesque Prince of Death, and Deathroot began spreading through the world.

The death of her firstborn drove Marika to shatter the Elden Ring with her hammer, fragmenting cosmic law itself. The Greater Will punished her by crucifying both Marika and Radagon within the Erdtree.
— The Shattering

The Shattering & The War

The shattered Ring's fragments became Great Runes, inherited by Marika's demigod offspring. "Tainted by their newfound strength," they warred against each other for dominance.

The final major battle occurred at Aeonia in Caelid between Radahn and Malenia. They fought to a stalemate until Malenia unleashed her first Scarlet Aeonia, devastating the entire region. The rot poisoned Radahn so deeply he lost his mind, becoming a feral beast. During Malenia's absence, Mohg kidnapped Miquella from his cocoon, taking him to Mohgwyn Palace.

The Age Before the Erdtree

The Greater Will fractures the One Great. Ancient Dragons rule under Dragonlord Placidusax. Fire Giants worship the Fell God.

The Rise of Marika

The Elden Beast arrives carrying the Elden Ring. Marika becomes its vessel and removes the Rune of Death, founding the Golden Order.

The Golden Age

Wars against Giants, dragons, and the Eternal Cities. Radagon marries Rennala, then returns to become Marika's consort.

The Night of Black Knives

Ranni orchestrates Godwyn's assassination. Marika shatters the Elden Ring. Both she and Radagon are imprisoned within the Erdtree.

The Shattering War

Demigods war for Great Runes. Radahn vs Malenia devastates Caelid. Mohg takes Miquella. No victor emerges—stalemate.

Return of the Tarnished

Grace calls the exiled Tarnished back to collect Great Runes and claim the Elden Throne.

The Tarnished

Tarnished are individuals who lost the Erdtree's grace and were banished from the Lands Between. The original Tarnished were Godfrey and his warrior kinfolk, whose eyes literally lost their golden hue when grace departed.

Grace manifests as golden light in one's eyes and at Sites of Grace, providing guidance, healing, and resurrection. When Marika stripped Godfrey of grace, she promised: "Return to the Lands Between, wage war, and brandish the Elden Ring. Grow strong in the face of death."

After the Shattering, grace was extended to the Tarnished once more, beckoning them back to collect Great Runes, defeat corrupted demigods, and become Elden Lord.

The Six Endings

Every ending responds to the failures of the Golden Order differently—restoration, reform, liberation, or annihilation.

Age of Fracture

The default ending. Mend the Elden Ring as-is and become Elden Lord, restoring the Golden Order with all its original flaws.

Age of Duskborn

Fia's ending. Embed the principle of life within Death into Order. Natural death returns, and Those Who Live in Death are legitimized.

Blessing of Despair

Dung Eater's ending. Make everyone an Omen—souls can no longer return to the Erdtree. If everyone is cursed, none can be scorned.

Age of Order

Goldmask's ending. Create a purified Golden Order that removes divine whim but may sacrifice freedom for cold perfectionism.

Age of Stars

Ranni's ending. Supplant Marika with an Order of moon and stars. A thousand year voyage under the Moon, removing all divine oversight.

Lord of Frenzied Flame

Become Lord of the Frenzied Flame, burning everything and returning existence to primordial chaos. Total annihilation.
